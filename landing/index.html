Privacy Policy

Last Updated: June 29, 2026

1. Overview and Core Principle

StreamFolio ("the Application") is built from the ground up to respect user privacy. We do not collect, store, transmit, or share any personal data. The Application functions primarily as a local client that fetches public RSS/Atom XML feeds directly from publisher websites and parses them locally on your device.

2. On-Device Local Processing

All core actions are performed locally on your physical device, ensuring maximum security and data privacy:

  • Local Caching: Article entries, bookmarks, custom RSS URLs, and categories are saved directly in a local database (Android Room SQLite) on your device. This data never leaves your device.
  • On-Device AI Summarization: Article text summarization (coming soon in v1.2) is performed strictly locally using Google's ML Kit GenAI Summarization API (powered by Gemini Nano and the AICore system service). The full text of the articles is processed entirely on your physical hardware. No article bodies, query parameters, or parsed logs are transmitted to any cloud servers for summarization.
  • Text-to-Speech (TTS): Article listening uses the built-in Android TextToSpeech engine on your device.

3. Third-Party Websites and Services

When you click on an article to read the full story in Web View mode, the Application loads the publisher's public webpage within a standard Android WebView component. We do not control and are not responsible for the privacy practices, tracking cookies, or contents of third-party publisher websites. We recommend reviewing the privacy statements of those websites.

4. Network Access and Operations

The Application requests standard Internet permissions solely to download the XML feeds of publishers you select. We do not use proprietary tracking metrics or analytics engines (such as Google Analytics or Firebase Analytics) inside the client app.

5. Policy Revisions

We may update this Privacy Policy to reflect changes in local device capabilities. Continued use of the Application signifies your agreement to local-only processing principles.
